What this is

I made RumboRumbo — Spanish and nautical for heading, course, direction. Where things are going. because I got tired of noise: clickbait, outrage loops, ads dressed as news. People have stopped reading news altogether. Not from lack of interest. From exhaustion.

Rumbo is the short version. Not breaking news — what is actually shifting in the world, and why it matters.

How it works

Rumbo takes no sides. No country, no bloc, no ideology. Things are described by what they do, not whether they are right.

Each edition is generated by an AI language modelCurrently Claude by Anthropic — claude.ai. AI makes mistakes, like any news source. But there's no spin, no agenda, no ads. The prompt is public. Nothing is hidden.

Using Rumbo

Each edition has a few global items picked for what actually changes things, not how loud they were. Pick a regional focus and a local item is added when something genuinely significant happened — most days nothing qualifies, which is fine.

The Meanwhile section has four shorter items — things worth knowing that aren't part of the daily news cycle. One each from Culture, Science, Wellbeing, and Worldviews. Hover the category label for a definition.

The ↗ arrow next to each item opens a search for that storyUses DuckDuckGo — a privacy-focused search engine that doesn't track you — duckduckgo.com. The source count (~N sources) is a rough estimate of how many separate outlets covered the story independently — more means wider reporting, not that it's accurate. Hover for details.

Rumbo updates daily. Errors happen. It's a starting point, not the last word.
