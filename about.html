What this is

A world intelligence brief with nothing to hide.

The idea

Most news is optimised for clicks, time on screen, or confirming what you already believe. Rumbo.WTF is built for the opposite: what actually shifted today, why it matters, and nothing else.

Each edition covers a small number of developments — sometimes five, sometimes two — selected because they have real consequences for how the world works, not because they are loud or dramatic. The format is the same every time. The language is plain. There are no ads, no sponsors, no agenda.

How we see the world

We try to approach the news the way an observer arriving on Earth for the first time would — without allegiance to any country, bloc, religion, or ideology. There is no home team.

Nation-states, alliances, and belief systems are described by what they do and what interests they serve. Religions and ideologies are treated as thought-systems with histories and consequences — the same lens applied to everything else. Passive language that hides who did what is avoided. If forces killed people, we say which forces.

How it is made

Each edition is generated using Claude, an AI model made by Anthropic, running a structured prompt against fresh sources from major international news outlets. The prompt used to generate each edition is shown in full below — nothing is hidden about how this works.

A confidence score appears in the footer of each edition. It reflects how well-sourced that edition is, not how certain we are about the world. Uncertainty is the honest default.

What this costs

Nothing. No ads, no sponsors, no promoted content. If that changes, it will be stated plainly at the top of the edition it changes in.

Testing phase. Rumbo.WTF is currently in early testing. Errors can and will occur. AI generation is fast but not infallible — treat everything here as a starting point, not a final word. Your critical thinking is not optional.
