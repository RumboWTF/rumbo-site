What this is

I made RumboRumbo — Spanish and nautical for heading, course, direction. Where things are going. because I got tired of noise. People have stopped reading news not from lack of interest, but exhaustion.

Rumbo is not breaking news. It's a short daily brief on what is actually shifting in the world.

How it works

Rumbo takes no sides. No country, no bloc, no ideology. Things are described by what they do, not whether they are right.

Each edition is generated by an AI language modelCurrently Claude by Anthropic — claude.ai. AI makes mistakes, like any news source. But there's no spin, no agenda, no ads. The prompt is public. Nothing is hidden.

Using Rumbo

Each edition has a few global items picked for signal, not volume. Pick a regional focus and a local item is added when something worth knowing happened — most days nothing qualifies.

The ↗ arrow opens a search for that storyUses DuckDuckGo — a privacy-focused search engine that doesn't track you — duckduckgo.com. The source count (~N) is a rough estimate of independent outlets — more means wider reporting, not that it's accurate.

Rumbo updates daily. Errors happen. It's a starting point, not the last word.
