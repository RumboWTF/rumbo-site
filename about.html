What this is

I made RumboRumbo — Spanish and nautical for heading, course, direction. Where things are going. because I got tired of noise: clickbait, outrage loops, ads dressed as news. People have stopped reading news altogether — not from lack of interest, but exhaustion.

Rumbo gives the minimum effective dose. Not breaking news, but what is actually shifting in the world. The big picture in five minutes.

How it works

Rumbo has no home team. No allegiance to country, bloc, religion, or ideology. Events and actors are described by what they do and who they serve, not whether they are right.

Each edition is generated by an AI language model (currently Claude by Anthropic). AI makes mistakes, like any news source. But Rumbo has no spin, no agenda, no ads, no pressure to keep you reading. The prompt is public, sources vary by day — nothing is hidden.

Using Rumbo

Each edition contains 3–4 global items selected for broader consequences, not headline volume. If you choose a regional focus in the footer, up to two local items are added — marked with a grey dot (global items carry a gold dot).

The Meanwhile section has four lighter items — one each from Culture, Science & tech, Wellbeing, and Worldviews. Hover the category label for a definition.

The ↗ arrow next to each item opens a DuckDuckGo search for that story. The source count (~N sources) is an estimate of independent reporting clusters — more sources means wider coverage, not that the story is accurate. Hover for details.

Rumbo updates at least once daily. Errors will occur. Think independently.
