01 — Situation

We live in the most informed era in history. And yet.

News consumption is at all-time highs. So is the feeling of being overwhelmed, manipulated, and still not quite sure what actually matters.

We're not tuning out from lack of interest. We're tuning out from exhaustion.

02 — Complication

News isn't broken. It's working exactly as designed.

Clicks, time-on-site, outrage, return visits. Ad-funded news has incentives that are structurally misaligned with informing you.

Algorithms reward loudness over consequence. Headlines are written for reaction, not comprehension. The stories that dominate coverage are rarely the ones that shift the world.

There is no neutral observer. Every outlet answers to someone.

Clickbait  ·  Outrage loops  ·  Ads dressed as news  ·  Domestic bias  ·  Agenda  ·  Volume

04 — Fair questions

What Rumbo is not.

Will I get breaking news?
No. Rumbo updates once a day. If something breaks mid-afternoon, you won't see it until tomorrow — and only if it matters beyond the moment.
Sports, entertainment, markets?
Normally not. The main feed covers developments that actually change things — what's shifting politically, economically, geographically. The Meanwhile section occasionally includes a cultural or sporting moment worth knowing, but scores and stock prices are out of scope.
Can I trust AI-generated news?
Trust it the way you'd trust one well-meaning journalist with no editor. Mistakes happen. The prompt is public. No hidden agenda is possible if the instructions are visible.
So what is it actually for?
At the end of the year, you won't be surprised by how the world moved. You'll have caught the slow shifts most people missed because they were reading headlines.
