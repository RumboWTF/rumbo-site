UN declares transatlantic slavery gravest crime against humanity
The General Assembly adopted a resolution declaring the trafficking of enslaved Africans the gravest crime against humanity and calling for reparations. One hundred twenty-three countries supported the resolution, while three opposed it including the US and Israel, and 52 abstained including UK and EU countries.

Olympics mandates genetic testing for all women athletes
The International Olympic Committee will require all athletes who want to participate in women's events to undergo genetic testing, with the policy taking effect for the 2028 Summer Games in Los Angeles. Only biological female athletes would be eligible for women's events from Los Angeles 2028 onwards, following a one-time gene test designed to identify male sex development.

Rapper becomes Nepal's youngest prime minister after uprising
Balendra Shah was sworn in after his three-year-old party won 182 seats in the 275-member parliament in the March 5 vote, the first election since anticorruption Gen Z-led protests in which 76 people were killed. The 35-year-old is Nepal's youngest prime minister in decades and the first Madhesi—people of the southern plains bordering India—to lead the Himalayan nation.

Philippines declares world's first national energy emergency
President Marcos declared a state of national energy emergency saying the Philippines had enough crude oil supply until June 30, making the Philippines the first country in the world to declare an energy emergency. The emergency declaration came as Philippine transport workers, commuters and consumer groups plan to hold a two-day strike to protest the increase in fuel prices.
