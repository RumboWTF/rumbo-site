Australia urges fuel conservation as Strait of Hormuz closure strains supply
Australia's prime minister urged citizens to take public transportation and avoid panic buying, warning that fuel should be saved for farmers and critical industries. While Australia imports little crude directly from the Middle East, up to 80 percent of its refined fuel comes via Asian refineries dependent on routes through the Strait of Hormuz.
